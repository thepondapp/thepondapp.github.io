Frequently Asked Questions


General FAQ


Why do I have to put in my ID to create an account?

At The Pond, we believe that the best connections are made with real people. We use a combination of a photo ID and a selfie to verify each user's identity and ensure that every person you connect with is who they say they are.

We do not store or share these images and we delete them as soon as we are able to verify your identity.

How does The Pond verify my data?

There are three methods we use to verify information:

1) Pictures of documents (photo ID, transcripts, diplomas, etc.)
2) Third-party websites (Credit Karma, College Board, etc.)
3) Email addresses (alumni, company, etc.)

How does The Pond keep my data secure?

The Pond uses user-controlled encryption keys to secure private information. Document images are retained only as long as it takes for us to verify them and are then deleted. We do not retain or view any login information used to pull data from a third-party website. The only information that is public is information that users choose to make public.

Why do I need to put in my data?

The Pond creates high-value communities by matching users with groups designed specifically for them. Entering more data will allow users to access more relevant, higher-value communities.

How long does it take to verify my information?

Third-party website verification takes one to five minutes. Email verification links are sent within five minutes. Document verification can take up to 24 hours.

As a group organizer, why do I want to build a community on The Pond?

The Pond is designed to support group organizers by enabling them to build better communities. Organizers can find the most relevant members and create targeted groups and sub-groups to facilitate more valuable connections and curate better content.

If you are a group organizer and have ideas on how our app could better serve you, please send us a message below or post a suggestion in our Telegram chat.
